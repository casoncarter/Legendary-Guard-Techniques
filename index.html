Ball-Handling Package

Dribbling — Pace, Control & Deception

Blend Curry’s pace changes, Murray’s hesitation, Nash’s probes, Jason Williams’ creativity, Trae’s shield dribble, and Wojo’s low stance to build a complete handle.

Steph Curry – Float Dribble
Jamal Murray – Hang Hesitation
Jason Williams – Creative Handles
Steve Nash – Continuous Probe
Trae Young – Trailing Dribble
Wojo – Low Stance Control

Key Teaching Points

  • Float dribble (Curry): small hang to read the defender before changing speed.
  • Hang hesitation to attack (Murray): sell the pause, then explode into a long stride.
  • Live-dribble passing (Jason Williams): every dribble is a threat to deliver a pass.
  • Continuous probe (Nash): keep the dribble alive under the rim, never panic pick-ups.
  • Shield & trailing dribble (Trae): keep the ball behind your body vs pressure.
  • Low stance (Wojo): hips low, chest up, ball below the knee in traffic.

Drill Menu

  • Two-Ball Pound + Float – one ball low & hard, the other floats at hip height.
  • Hang Hesitation Cone Series – at each cone, sell a pause then attack downhill.
  • Continuous Paint Dribble – circle the lane 8–10 seconds without picking up.
  • Shield Dribble vs Pad – have a partner bump you while you protect the ball.
  • Mirror Low-Stance Dribble – partner moves laterally, you mirror while dribbling.

Weekly & Monthly Plan

Use this structure to repeat high-quality reps. Adjust volume for age, season, and game schedule.

Weekly Rhythm

6-Day Guard Training Schedule

Aim for 60–90 minutes per day. Add warm-up and cool-down as needed.

Day Focus Details (Player Inspiration)
Monday Dribbling + Shooting Curry float dribbles, Murray hesitations, form shooting + Curry relocation threes.
Tuesday Passing + Defense Nash vision drills, Jason Williams creative passes, Payton pressure slides, Wojo closeouts.
Wednesday Movement + Conditioning Curry relocation, Nash cut-after-pass, lane sprints, defensive slide ladders.
Thursday Pick & Roll + Shooting Murray snake dribble, Trae floater + deep pull-ups, Nash pocket passes.
Friday Scrimmage + Film Controlled 3v3/5v5 with rules (e.g., must use PnR), then 20–30 min film of the week’s focus player.
Saturday Long Skill Session Combine all skills: handle into shot, shot fake into drive, drive into kick-out pass, finish in traffic.

Sunday = recovery, light shooting, stretching, and leadership reflection using the Coach K section below.

Monthly Emphasis

4-Week Development Cycle

Recycle this 4-week block throughout the year; each time, raise your standards.

Week Theme
Week 1 Handle & Footwork – Curry/Murray handle, Nash probes, Wojo stance, jump-stop & stride-stop footwork.
Week 2 Shooting & Movement – Curry relocation, Trae range, Murray midrange; cutting and screening angles.
Week 3 Passing & PnR – Nash angles, Jason Williams creativity, Trae lobs, advanced PnR reads.
Week 4 Defense & Leadership – Payton pressure, Wojo charges, Curry off-ball IQ, Coach K “standards” and “next play.”

Track notes each week: what improved, what still feels shaky, and how your mindset evolved.

Illustrated Drill Book

Simple half-court diagrams in text form. You can redraw these on a whiteboard or into a play-diagram app. 

Top view (3 players)
   Wing           Top           Corner

   A ●           B ●           C ●

1. A passes to B, cuts to corner.
2. B swings to C.
3. A sprints to fill open spot for 3.
  • Rotate A → B → C every few reps.
  • Focus on foot prep before the catch.
  • Add a defender later to chase A through the relocation.
Side view – half court lane

   O = ballhandler
   X = cone/imaginary help

   O dribbles in, loops under rim,
   exits on opposite side without picking up.

   X   X
    \ /
     O
  • Stay low, eyes up, keep your dribble alive under the rim.
  • Add a passing option to the corner or dunker spot.
  • Great for learning patience in the paint.
Top view – high PnR

     X2
          X5
   O1   ● (screener)
          |
         Rim

1. O1 uses screen, defender X5 steps up.
2. Roller ● dives.
3. O1 slips ball into pocket before help rotates.
  • Work on timing the pass as the big flips their hips.
  • Use both bounce and chest pocket passes.
  • Add a weak-side shooter to skip pass if tagger helps.
Side view – sideline channel drill

Sideline
| X  <- defender slides, chest to dribbler
| O  <- ballhandler pushes up the floor
|

1. Defender X forces O to weaker hand.
2. Stay in front, no reaching.
3. Slide, cut off angles, contain to sideline.
  • Short bursts: 3–4 dribbles, then reset.
  • Emphasize chest contact, not hands.
  • Switch roles every rep to feel both sides.

Coach K Leadership Lab

This section is about approach, attitude, and leadership. Use it as your mental training block at the end of each day.

Mindset & Standards

“Standards, Not Just Rules”

Coach K’s teams were built on shared standards: how you talk, how you compete, and how you treat teammates. As a guard, you set the tone.

Next Play Mentality
Communicate Constantly
Selfless Competitiveness
Arrive Early, Stay Late
Hold Teammates Accountable

Daily Leadership Checklist

  • Next Play – After every mistake or big play, reset your body language within 3 seconds.
  • Communication – Call out screens, matchups, and coverages every possession you’re on the floor.
  • Selfless Energy – Celebrate teammates’ success louder than your own.
  • Preparation = Confidence – Know your workout block before you step on the court.
  • Standards Talk – If a teammate’s effort slips, talk to them privately and directly.

Weekly Reflection Prompts

  • When did I live our team standards this week?
  • Where did my body language slip?
  • Who did I encourage? Who can I encourage next week?
  • What did I prepare well for? What did I wing?

“Leadership is not about you; it’s about the people you serve. The moment you understand that, you start to lead the way your team needs you to.”

— Inspired by Coach K’s leadership philosophy

Use your guard spot to:

  • Organize huddles before free throws and after timeouts.
  • Call mini “reset” huddles when momentum swings either way.
  • Match your voice with your effort and defense.

PLAYER MENTORS

Study these guards to build your own game. Use their strengths as models:
handles, shooting, pick-and-roll reads, vision, creativity, defense, and leadership.

Steph Curry

Shooting · Off-ball Movement

  • Consistent footwork and balance into every shot.
  • Relocation threes after passing or driving.
  • Elite conditioning to move without the ball all game.

Jamal Murray

Hesi · Pick-and-Roll Scoring

  • Signature "hang" hesitation to freeze defenders.
  • Snake dribble to create midrange or floater space.
  • Three-level scoring out of screens.

Steve Nash

Vision · Passing Angles

  • Keep the dribble alive in the paint.
  • Find skip, pocket, and drop-off passes early.
  • Body control finishing over size.

Trae Young

Floater · Deep Range · Playmaking

  • Elite floater game to punish drop coverage.
  • Unlimited deep range to stretch defenses and create driving lanes.
  • Advanced pick-and-roll reads: lobs, skips, pocket passes.

Max Abmas

Deep Range · Pace Control · Small-Guard Craft

  • Elite deep-range shooting to stretch defenses 28–30+ feet.
  • Advanced pace control: hesitations, pauses, and sudden bursts.
  • Small-guard finishing craft: floaters, off-foot layups, creative angles.
  • High pick-and-roll efficiency with quick decision-making.
  • Clutch scorer with fearless “next shot” mentality.

Jason Williams

Creativity · Live-Dribble Passing

  • Play with flair while still making the simple read.
  • Develop live-dribble passing with both hands.
  • Create angles that open scoring for teammates.

Gary Payton

On-ball Defense · Pressure

  • Relentless point-of-attack pressure.
  • Force ball handlers into uncomfortable angles.
  • Use voice, physicality, and timing to disrupt.

Steve Wojciechowski

Stance · Charges · Leadership

  • Elite stance discipline and defensive detail.
  • Take charges and make momentum-turning plays.
  • Lead through communication and toughness.

SKILL SYSTEM

Core technical package for guards: dribbling, shooting, passing, movement without the ball, defense, and pick-and-roll, all modeled off the players above.

Handles

Dribbling Mechanics & Ball Control

Build a guard handle that works against real pressure: float dribbles to change pace, hesitations to freeze defenders, Nash-style probes in the paint, and escape techniques for traps like Trae Young.

1. Float Dribble (Change of Pace)

  • Start in a relaxed stance, ball in your strong hand.
  • Push the ball out slightly in front of you, then “hang” it with a soft, controlled dribble.
  • Let your body rise up to sell that you might pull up or stop.
  • As the defender relaxes or stands up, explode past with a long, low push step.
  • Work both hands, and practice using the float into pull-ups, drives, and step-backs.
Float Dribble ▶
The Float Dribble: Why Every Player Needs This Use this to change pace, keep defenders guessing, and set up your next move.

2. Hesitation Dribble (Jamal Murray)

  • Attack downhill, then let the ball “hang” at your side as you slightly rise up.
  • Eyes up at the rim as if you might shoot or pull up to force the defender to react.
  • As soon as the defender relaxes or shifts, drop your hips and explode past with one hard dribble.
  • Mix in hesitations into step-back threes, pull-ups, and drives to both hands.
Jamal Murray ▶
Jamal Murray Basketball Moves For Guards Study his hang dribble, hesitation timing, and change of speed out of the pick-and-roll.

3. Nash Dribble (Paint Probe)

  • Use a tight, controlled dribble to circle under the basket without picking the ball up.
  • Keep your eyes scanning: corner shooters, dunker spot, roller, and weak-side help.
  • If nothing is open, keep dribbling out the other side and re-attack—do not force a shot.
  • This move turns the paint into your “office” where you read the defense while still keeping your dribble.
Steve Nash ▶
Master the Nash Dribble | Become a Paint Playmaker Learn how to probe with the dribble and create layups, kick-outs, and dump-offs.

4. Escaping Traps (Trae Young)

  • When a trap comes, do not pick up your dribble unless you have a clear pass.
  • Use retreat dribbles (backward dribble) to pull the traps away from the basket.
  • Turn your body to shield the ball, using your outside leg and hip as protection.
  • See over the trap to find the open teammate—usually one pass away or diagonally opposite.

Shooting

Shooting: Footwork, Pull-Ups, and Range

Build a repeatable shot: clean footwork, balanced pull-ups, and extended range without losing form.

1. Shot Preparation & Footwork

  • Land on two feet or 1–2 into every catch so you’re balanced and ready.
  • Hands and eyes up before the ball arrives—be a receiver, not a watcher.
  • Lock in your follow-through and hold your finish for one count.
Shot Prep ▶
Shot Preparation & Footwork Dial in your base and rhythm so every shot starts the same way.

2. Pull-Up Jumpers

  • Attack in a straight line, then plant into a 1–2 or two-foot stop.
  • Stay low into your stop so the shot flows up instead of drifting sideways.
  • Work pull-ups going left and right from the same starting move.
Pull-Ups ▶
Pull-Up Jumper Progression Build a game-speed pull-up that works from all three levels.

3. Range with Balance

  • Back your feet up gradually—do not change your form to “push” the ball.
  • Keep your shot straight by keeping your follow-through on the same line.
  • Stay in your makes and watch the ball go through before you relax.
Range ▶
Extending Your Range with Balance Learn how to stretch your range without sacrificing mechanics.
Playmaking

Passing & Playmaking

Turn your handle and vision into easy shots for teammates: simple reads, on-time passes, and strong decisions.

1. On-Time, On-Target Passing

  • Throw passes at chest height or directly into a shooter’s pocket.
  • Hit teammates as they are getting open, not after they’re standing still.
  • Use your off hand for simple push passes and skips.
Fundamentals ▶
Passing Fundamentals for Guards Build touch and accuracy with simple drills you can repeat every workout.

2. Drive & Kick Reads

  • Jump stop in the paint so you can see the floor without traveling.
  • Read the low man and the strong-side help before you leave your feet.
  • Kick to the first open teammate instead of hunting for the “fancy” pass.
Drive & Kick ▶
Drive & Kick Progression Practice seeing the help and finding shooters on time.

3. Advanced Passing Reads

  • Use skips when the weak-side defender tags the roller.
  • Throw pocket passes only when you can see a clean window.
  • Keep your turnovers low: simple is better than spectacular.
Advanced Reads ▶
Advanced Passing Reads Layer in skips, pocket passes, and late reads as you improve.
Off-Ball

Movement Without the Ball

Become a guard who is dangerous even when you’re not dribbling: cutting, relocating, and using screens.

1. Cuts for Easy Buckets

  • Time your cuts when your defender turns their head or ball-watches.
  • Use backdoor cuts when your defender is overplaying the passing lane.
  • Finish off two feet through contact at the rim.
Cuts ▶
5 Game-Ready Cuts Learn when and how to cut for easy layups and open threes.

2. Relocation & Spacing

  • After you pass, move to a new window instead of standing still.
  • Slide along the three-point line to stay in your passer’s vision.
  • Keep the paint clear for drives and post touches.
Relocation ▶
Relocation Shooting & Spacing Learn how to relocate after passing and get open shots.

3. Using Off-Ball Screens

  • Set your defender up by walking them into the screen.
  • Read the defender: curl, fade, or pop depending on how they chase.
  • Catch ready to shoot, drive, or swing to the next action.
Screens ▶
Using Off-Ball Screens Study how great shooters use screens to create separation.
Defense

On-Ball Defense & Competing

Become a guard coaches trust to guard the ball, talk, and finish possessions.

1. Stance & Slides

  • Wide base, hips low, chest up, and hands active.
  • Push off your back foot to slide—do not click your heels together.
  • Finish each rep with a contest and a two-hand rebound.
Stance ▶
Defensive Stance & Slides Master your stance and movement to stay in front of drivers.

2. On-Ball Defense

  • Angle your body to push the ball toward help, not the middle of the floor.
  • Use chest and feet, not hands, to stay in front.
  • Compete to win the first two steps every time the ball changes direction.
On-Ball ▶
On-Ball Defense Drills Train your slides, cut-offs, and contests vs live ball handlers.

3. Compete on Every Possession

  • Talk early and often: ball, help, screen, and switches.
  • Dive for loose balls and sprint back after every miss or turnover.
  • Finish plays with a rebound or a clean box-out.
Compete ▶
Competing on Defense See how elite guards turn defense into a competitive advantage.
Pick & Roll

Pick & Roll Reads for Guards

Learn how to use ball screens to create shots for yourself and teammates with three core reads.

1. Turn the Corner vs. Drop Coverage

  • Come off the screen tight, with your inside shoulder almost brushing the screener.
  • Attack the big’s outside hip—make them decide between you and the roller.
  • If the big stays back, get downhill for a layup, floater, or paint touch.
Turn the Corner ▶
Turning the Corner vs Drop See how great guards punish drop coverage by getting downhill.

2. Snake Dribble

  • Start like you’re turning the corner, then cross back in front of the screener.
  • Use your body to shield the defender on your back while you change angles.
  • Look for pull-ups, floaters, or pocket passes as you snake across the lane.
Snake ▶
Snake Dribble in the Pick & Roll Learn how to keep your dribble alive and change angles to create space.

3. Rejecting the Screen

  • Sell that you’re going to use the screen by taking a step toward it.
  • As the defender cheats over the top, cross or rip the ball away from the screen.
  • Attack the open space hard—no hesitation once you reject.
Reject ▶
Rejecting the Screen Study how guards read the defender and attack away from the screen.

Weekly & Monthly Plan

Use this structure to repeat high-quality reps. Adjust volume for age, season, and game schedule.

Weekly Rhythm

6-Day Guard Training Schedule

Aim for 60–90 minutes per day. Add warm-up and cool-down as needed.

Day Focus Details (Player Inspiration)
Monday Dribbling + Shooting Curry float dribbles, Murray hesitations, form shooting + Curry relocation threes.
Tuesday Passing + Defense Nash vision drills, Jason Williams creative passes, Payton pressure slides, Wojo closeouts.
Wednesday Movement + Conditioning Curry relocation, Nash cut-after-pass, lane sprints, defensive slide ladders.
Thursday Pick & Roll + Shooting Murray snake dribble, Trae floater + deep pull-ups, Nash pocket passes.
Friday Scrimmage + Film Controlled 3v3/5v5 with rules (e.g., must use PnR), then 20–30 min film of the week’s focus player.
Saturday Long Skill Session Combine all skills: handle into shot, shot fake into drive, drive into kick-out pass, finish in traffic.

Sunday = recovery, light shooting, stretching, and leadership reflection using the Coach K section below.

Monthly Emphasis

4-Week Development Cycle

Recycle this 4-week block throughout the year; each time, raise your standards.

Week Theme
Week 1 Handle & Footwork – Curry/Murray handle, Nash probes, Wojo stance, jump-stop & stride-stop footwork.
Week 2 Shooting & Movement – Curry relocation, Trae range, Murray midrange; cutting and screening angles.
Week 3 Passing & PnR – Nash angles, Jason Williams creativity, Trae lobs, advanced PnR reads.
Week 4 Defense & Leadership – Payton pressure, Wojo charges, Curry off-ball IQ, Coach K “standards” and “next play.”

Track notes each week: what improved, what still feels shaky, and how your mindset evolved.

DRILL BOOK

Core guard drills mapped to the player mentors above. Each drill has a clear focus, simple constraints, and a video clip to model footwork, pacing, and decision-making.

Steph Curry: Form & Footwork

One-motion form, balanced base, and repeatable footwork.

  • Start with stationary form shots: 10 makes from each side and middle.
  • Progress to one-two and hop footwork from the midrange into the three.
  • Finish with 5 “game-speed” makes from each spot with no pauses in between.
Watch Curry form breakdown ▶

Steph Curry: Off-the-Dribble Threes

Pull-ups, rhythm step-backs, and range under control.

  • Use cones to mark two pull-up spots and one step-back spot behind the arc.
  • Attack with 1–2 hard dribbles into a balanced pull-up; hold your follow-through.
  • Track “makes in a row” to build pressure on your last few reps.
Watch off-the-dribble 3s ▶

Steph Curry: Relocation Shooting

Pass, relocate, and re-space into open threes.

  • Start in the slot, pass to imaginary teammate (coach or wall), then relocate to corner.
  • Catch on the move with your hands and feet ready; shoot in rhythm.
  • Alternate slot-to-corner and corner-to-wing for 20–30 total makes.
Watch relocation examples ▶

Steve Nash: Paint Probe

Turn the paint into your office without picking up your dribble.

  • Start on the wing, drive into the paint and dribble under the rim without stopping.
  • Circle under the basket, keep your eyes up for imaginary kick-outs and dump-offs.
  • On coach’s call (“kick” or “finish”), either re-attack or pull up in the lane.
Watch Nash probe clip ▶

Gary Payton: On-Ball Defense

Low stance, active hands, and controlled physicality.

  • Mirror slides: shuffle laterally from sideline to lane line without crossing your feet.
  • Add “cut-off” reps where you beat the offensive player to the spot and chest up.
  • Finish with 30 seconds live one-on-one, forcing tough, contested jumpers.
Watch Payton defensive clips ▶

Gary Payton: Contest Without Fouling

Close hard, stop under control, challenge straight up.

  • From the paint, sprint out to a shooter on the wing, then short-stutter into your stance.
  • Hand up on the shooting side; jump straight up, not into the shooter.
  • Track “perfect contests” where you arrive on balance and force a miss.
Watch contest highlights ▶

Wojo: Floor-Slap Compete Drill

Energy, communication, and pride in every possession.

  • Slap the floor, call out the ball-handler and the help positions.
  • Defend for 10–15 second bursts, staying low the entire possession.
  • Rotate with a teammate so one plays on-ball, one plays help each rep.
Watch Wojo intensity ▶

Jamal Murray: Left-Hand Attack

Confident left-hand dribble into pull-ups, finishes, and kick-outs.

  • Start on the right wing, attack downhill with your left hand only.
  • Finish 5 reps as pull-up jumpers, 5 as floaters, and 5 at the rim through contact.
  • On every drive, chin the ball and land on two feet ready to pass or pivot.
Watch Murray left-hand drives ▶

Trae Young: Pick-and-Roll Reads

Manipulate the big, punish the help, and always find an answer.

  • Use a cone or chair as the screener; come off shoulder-to-hip with the screen.
  • Read the defender: pull up if they go under, attack the big if they drop, hit the roller if they help.
  • Track correct reads, not just makes, to build decision-making under pressure.

COACH K LEADERSHIP LAB

This section is about approach, attitude, and leadership. Use it as your mental training block at the end of each day.

Mindset & Standards

“Standards, Not Just Rules”

Coach K’s teams were built on shared standards: how you talk, how you compete, and how you treat teammates. As a guard, you set the tone for everyone else.

Next Play Mentality
Communicate Constantly
Selfless Competitiveness
Arrive Early, Stay Late
Hold Teammates Accountable

Daily Leadership Checklist

  • Next Play – After every mistake or big play, reset your body language within 3 seconds.
  • Communication – Call out screens, matchups, and coverages every possession you’re on the floor.
  • Selfless Energy – Celebrate teammates’ success louder than your own.
  • Preparation = Confidence – Know your workout block before you step on the court.
  • Standards Talk – If a teammate’s effort slips, talk to them privately and directly.

Weekly Reflection Prompts

  • When did I live our team standards this week?
  • Where did my body language slip?
  • Who did I encourage? Who can I encourage next week?
  • What did I prepare well for? What did I just wing?

Use your guard spot to:

  • Organize huddles before free throws and after timeouts.
  • Call mini “reset” huddles when momentum swings either way.
  • Match your voice with your effort and defense.
